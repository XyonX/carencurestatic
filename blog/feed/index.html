1) What Is Heart Failure?

Heart failure is a condition in which the heart isn’t pumping the way it should be pumping. Our body depends on the pumping mechanism of the heart to deliver oxygen and nutrient-rich blood to the body cells. When the heart is weakened and can’t supply the cells with enough blood, then the heart failure condition takes place. The narrowed arteries or high blood pressure leaves the heart weak or stiff and pump efficiently.

2) What Are The Symptoms That You May Face During Heart Failure?

Here are some sign and symptoms that can be chronic or acute depending on your condition-

  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Shortness of breath
  • Swelling in legs, feet, ankles, abdomen
  • Increased need to urinate at night
  • Lack of appetite and nausea
  • Decreased alertness
  • Chest pain
  • Fainting
  • Irregular heartbeats

  • Infection
  • Trauma
  • Iatrogenic injury

وا حد) ما هو قصور القلب؟

فشل القلب هو حالة لا يضخ فيها القلب بالطريقة التي ينبغي أن يضخ فيها. يعتمد جسمنا على آلية ضخ القلب لتوصيل الأكسجين والدم الغني بالمغذيات إلى خلايا الجسم. عندما يضعف القلب ولا يستطيع إمداد الخلايا بالدم الكافي ، تحدث حالة فشل القلب. تضيق الشرايين أو ارتفاع ضغط الدم يترك القلب ضعيفًا أو متصلبًا ويضخه بكفاءة.

1) What is Valve Replacement Surgery?

The heart has four valves that normally allow blood to flow only in one direction throughout the heart. The valve is responsible for allowing the blood rich with nutrients to flow through the chamber of the heart. Each valve after the flow of blood is supposed to close completely after ushering in blood flow. The heart valve that is improperly working isn’t always able to close the valve as they should. Leaky valve instead of closing tightly, it may remain open, letting the blood flow backward. This condition is known as regurgitation.

When a heart valve is at risk it will show up certain signs like dizziness, fatigue, chest pain, shortness of breath, lightheadedness, etc. This is the reason heart valve replacement surgery is suggested to patients with a leaky heart valve. The condition can occur due to age-related changes, any disorder, infection, rheumatic fever, your overall health, your heart function, etc. In some people, the damage is too advanced in that case a total replacement to the affected valve is suggested.

2) What are the Types of Valve Replacement Surgery?

The valve used for the replacement is the artificial component that has the same purpose as a natural heart valve. The artificial valve can last for 10-20 years. The doctor will recommend the heart valve replacement surgery on the basis of age, overall health, patients’ ability to take anticoagulant medication, and the extent of the disease.

  • Aortic Valve Replacement : The aortic valve is located on the left side of the heart and serves as an outflow valve. With aortic valve replacement surgery, the survival rate is 94% for five years.
  • Mitral Valve Replacement : It is located on the left side of the heart and serves as the inflow valve. Surgery is suggested when the valve doesn’t fully open or closes completely. There is a 91 percent chance of survival for five years.
  • Double Valve Replacement : In this replacement, both the mitral and aortic valve or the entire left side of the heart valve is replaced.
  • Pulmonary Valve Replacement : In pulmonary valve replacement that carries blood to the lungs, it needs to be replaced due to stenosis, that restricts blood flow which is caused due to congenital defect, carcinoid syndrome, or infection.
