1) What do you know about Kidney Stones?

The kidney is a part of the body that is roughly the size of a small fist. The kidney filters the blood, and removes the wastes, keeps the level of electrolytes maintained, and controls the body fluid balance. The blood in the body passes several times a day through the kidney. This is the main function of the kidney.

At a certain condition, when your kidney makes urine, calcium, and oxalate (chemical) stick together, then the kidney stones are formed. The stones in the kidney are the hard deposits of minerals and salts that are formed inside your kidney. When the urine becomes more concentrated, and minerals to crystallize and stick together.

You can eventually feel the symptoms until the stones move into the ureter.

  • Blood in urine
  • Pus in the urine
  • Severe pain in the groin
  • Reduced amount of urine excreted
  • Urge to urinate
  • Burning sensation during urination

2) What is the Best Treatment for Kidney Stones?

The size of the stone determines the Treatment of kidney stones , whether it is causing you pain or else if it is blocking your urinary tract. When you visit a doctor, he will ask you certain questions, and tests will be performed like the urine test, x-ray, blood test, CT scan, etc. When the test results are positive, it will even show the size of stones that can be small or large.

If the size is small, then your doctor can prescribe you to take pain medicine and drink plenty of water or fluids to help the stone push through your urinary tract. And if the stones are large then, it may cause you blocking of the urinary tract that needs the treatment.

Ureteroscopy- It is a treatment option for kidney stone, that is used by the surgeon to remove the stone and break it into small pieces. The broken pieces are small enough to pass through the urinary tract.

Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy- This procedure is used in rare cases; the stones are removed from the kidney. You have to stay in the hospital for two to three days to recover from the treatment.
