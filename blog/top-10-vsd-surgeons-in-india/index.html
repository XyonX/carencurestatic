1) What should you know about VSD?

Basically, VSD stands for A Ventricular Septal Defect, which means a hole in the heart. VSD is a congenital heart defect that is present by birth. The hole that is known as a defect occurs in the wall of Septum that separates the hearts lower chambers and allows blood to pass to the right from the left of the heart. This oxygen-rich blood is then pumped back to the lungs instead of out to the body. This condition causes the heart to work harder.

2) What are the symptoms that you may feel in your child?

When there is a Ventricular Septal Defect , there are different signs and symptoms that the baby may face during the first few days, weeks or months. The symptoms mostly depend on the size and location of the hole. If VSD is small, it may not cause any symptoms, but if the hole is large then you may see your child is-

  • Poor eating, failure to thrive
  • Fast breathing or breathlessness
  • Tired quickly during attempts to feed
  • Starts sweating or crying with feeding
  • Gain weight at a slower rate
